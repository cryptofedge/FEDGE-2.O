# Royalties Guide — Music Rights, PROs & Revenue

## Two Types of Rights

### Master Rights
- Owned by whoever funded the recording (artist, label, or both)
- Generates: streaming royalties, sync fees, master licensing
- Paid to: master rights holder via distributor

### Publishing Rights (Songwriting)
- Owned by songwriter(s) and publisher
- Generates: performance royalties, mechanical royalties, sync fees
- Paid via PRO → publisher → songwriter

---

## PROs — Performing Rights Organizations

### ASCAP (American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers)
- URL: https://www.ascap.com
- 1.1+ million members — largest PRO in the US
- *Only not-for-profit PRO in the US* — puts creators first
- Collects performance royalties for: radio, TV, streaming, live venues, digital
- How to join: ascap.com/join
- Royalty search: https://www.ascap.com/repertory
- Cost: $50 one-time registration fee for songwriters
- Pays out: quarterly

### BMI (Broadcast Music Inc)
- URL: https://www.bmi.com
- 1.4+ million songwriters and publishers
- FREE to join for songwriters
- Collects performance royalties for: radio, TV, streaming, digital
- How to join: bmi.com/join
- Repertoire search: https://repertoire.bmi.com
- Pays out: quarterly

### SESAC
- Invite-only, smaller but known for faster and higher payouts
- URL: sesac.com

### SoundExchange
- Collects digital performance royalties (Pandora, SiriusXM, internet radio)
- Separate from ASCAP/BMI — register independently at soundexchange.com
- Free to register — both master and publishing get paid

---

## The MLC — Mechanical Licensing Collective
- URL: https://portal.themlc.com
- Collects *mechanical royalties* from digital streaming services (Spotify, Apple Music, etc.)
- Free to register — required for ALL songwriters/publishers to collect mechanicals
- US mechanical rate: ~$0.091 per download / stream unit
- Register every song before or shortly after release
- Replaces Harry Fox Agency as the US mechanical collection body

---

## Full Royalty Flow for a Song

```
Song streams on Spotify
    ↓
Master royalty → paid to distributor → paid to artist
Publishing royalty →
    Performance → ASCAP or BMI → publisher → songwriter
    Mechanical → The MLC → publisher → songwriter
Digital performance → SoundExchange → master owner + featured artist
```

## Sync Licensing
- Placement in TV, film, ads, games
- Fee range: $500 → $500,000+ depending on usage
- Requires licensing both master AND sync (publishing) rights
- Pitch to music supervisors and sync libraries:
  - Musicbed (musicbed.com)
  - Artlist (artlist.io)
  - Epidemic Sound (epidemicsound.com)
  - Musicosophia, Crucial Music, etc.

## Label Deal Types
| Deal | Pros | Cons |
|------|------|------|
| Traditional | Advance + full support | Label owns masters, heavy recoupment |
| 360 Deal | Full backing | Label takes % of ALL revenue |
| Joint Venture | Shared ownership | Less control |
| Licensing | Keep masters | Limited term, limited support |
| Distribution Only | Full control | No advance, no marketing |

**Red flags:** 100% recoupment before royalties, permanent master ownership, no creative approval, 7+ album options, no audit rights. Always get an entertainment attorney.
