# Royalties Guide — Music Rights & Revenue

## Two Types of Rights

### Master Rights
- Owned by: whoever funded the recording (artist, label, or both)
- Generates: streaming royalties, sync fees, master licensing
- Paid to: master rights holder via distributor

### Publishing Rights (Songwriting)
- Owned by: songwriter(s) and publisher
- Generates: performance royalties, mechanical royalties, sync fees
- Paid to: PRO (ASCAP, BMI, SESAC) → publisher → songwriter

---

## PROs (Performing Rights Organizations)
Register with one:
- **ASCAP** — ascap.com (US)
- **BMI** — bmi.com (US, free)
- **SESAC** — sesac.com (US, invite-only)
- **SoundExchange** — for digital performance royalties (non-interactive streams like Pandora)

Register every song before release.

---

## Mechanical Royalties
- Generated when a song is reproduced (streams, downloads, CDs)
- US mechanical rate: $0.091 per copy / 9.1 cents per stream unit
- Collected by: MLC (Mechanical Licensing Collective) in the US
- Register at: themlc.com

## Sync Licensing
- Placement in TV, film, ads, games, YouTube
- One-time fee: $500–$500,000+ depending on usage
- Requires licensing both master AND sync (publishing) rights
- Pitch to: music supervisors, sync libraries (Musicbed, Artlist, Epidemic Sound)

## Label Deal Types
| Deal | Pros | Cons |
|------|------|------|
| Traditional | Advance, full support | Label owns masters, recoups heavily |
| 360 Deal | Full backing | Label takes % of ALL revenue streams |
| Joint Venture | Shared ownership | Less control than indie |
| Licensing | Keep masters | Limited term, limited support |
| Distribution Only | Full control | No advance, no marketing support |

**Red flags in contracts:**
- Recoups 100%+ before royalties paid
- Ownership of masters forever
- No creative approval clause
- Options for 7+ albums
- No audit rights

Always have an entertainment attorney review before signing.
